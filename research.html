The University of Eastern Pangasinan (UEP) has signed a MOA with the Commission on Population and Development (CPD) for its first externally funded research project, titled “Patterns and Effects of Parental Guidance on Sexuality and Reproductive Health Concerns among Children of OFW Families.” Led by the Research, Innovation, Training, and Extension office under Dr. Jeofrey B. Abalos, the study examines parent-child communication on reproductive health and sets the foundation for UEP’s future research endeavors.

𝐔𝐄𝐏 𝐉𝐨𝐢𝐧𝐬 𝐂𝐏𝐒-𝐋𝐞𝐝 𝐁𝐞𝐧𝐜𝐡𝐦𝐚𝐫𝐤𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐕𝐢𝐬𝐢𝐭 𝐭𝐨 𝐔𝐏, 𝐒𝐩𝐚𝐫𝐤𝐬 𝐂𝐨𝐥𝐥𝐚𝐛𝐨𝐫𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐯𝐞 𝐎𝐩𝐩𝐨𝐫𝐭𝐮𝐧𝐢𝐭𝐢𝐞𝐬 𝐢𝐧 𝐑𝐞𝐬𝐞𝐚𝐫𝐜𝐡 𝐚𝐧𝐝 𝐈𝐧𝐧𝐨𝐯𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧

The Center for Pangasinan Studies (CPS) led a benchmarking visit to UP Diliman and UP Los Baños on July 3–4, 2025, with UEP representatives joining to explore research, publication, and extension best practices. The two-day activity featured discussions on faculty incentives, collaborative programs, and global partnerships, highlighted by presentations from key UPLB offices and a talk by Balik Scientist Dr. Elemer Mojica. The visit strengthened academic ties and opened opportunities for cooperation in research, ethics training, and institutional development, reaffirming CPS and UEP’s commitment to advancing Pangasinan’s academic landscape.

The University of Eastern Pangasinan (UEP) successfully held its first International Research Conference on March 18, 2025, highlighting innovation, technology, business, and education under the theme “Leveraging Empirical Research to Promote Society’s Well-being and Foster International Understanding.” The event featured keynote speaker Dr. Dahlia Simangan from Hiroshima University and showcased outstanding research presentations, with Mr. Glen S. Diwag winning first place. The conference affirmed UEP’s dedication to promoting global research excellence and societal advancement.

UEP attends two back-to-back international research conferences

The University of Eastern Pangasinan (UEP) participated in two international research conferences: the 6th Asian Population Association Conference in Nepal and the 1st KUUB Postgraduate by Research Colloquium in Malaysia. Led by Research Director Dr. Jeofrey Abalos, who presented and chaired sessions, UEP delegates showcased studies on topics such as sustainability, education, and innovation. The events gathered global researchers, promoting collaboration and strengthening UEP’s commitment to addressing global challenges through research and academic excellence.
